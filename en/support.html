FAQs

For Libre Softwares (LGX, etc.)

Q: Can I redistribute products distributed under a libre software license?

A: Only official stable binaries are been able to distributed without authorisation; in such cases, please note that they are not NJB authorised.
For licenses without a source code disclosure clause, such as the 3-clause BSD license, you do not need to include the source code.

Q: I modified a product distributed under a libre software license. Can I distribute it?

A: You can only distribute it if the NJB's trademark has been removed. This is necessary to prevent users from mistaking the derivative work as NJB authorised.

Q: How about screenshots?

A: The following fair uses for libre softwares don't require permission.

  • If a magazine or website has screenshots of our software or website
  • TV shows or movies has a screen of NJB products
  • Place screenshots in commercial publications, whether or not they focus on NJB products

Q: Are trademark restrictions inconsistent with the libre software principle?

A: That's not true. Many libre software licenses exclude trademark rights and are based on the principle of libre software. A trademark is a proof of quality that protects users.
Also see the mention of the Free Software Foundation (FSF).

Q: Isn't it inconsistent with the GPL to put logos, game assets, etc. under different licenses?

A: Not contradictory, since logos, wallpapers, game scenario scripts (such as those developed by ONScripter, Ren'Py, and other game engines with GPL or LGPL portions), images, and sound files are just data used for the program.(Please also refer to the "Can I quote materials in the product?" section of the "For Video Games" section.
Note that libre free software binaries developed by NJB in totally from scratch are distributed under the 3-clause BSD license, and in such cases this question does not hold in practice.

For CD-EXTRA

Q: What's CD-EXTRA?

A: A compact disc that combines music (CD-DA) and computer data in a single session. The specification is defined in the so-called Blue Book.A compact disc that combines music (CD-DA) and computer data in a single session. The specification is defined in the so-called Blue Book.

Q: Why does NJB release CD-EXTRAs?

A: This is because we don't want to waste extra space on a single or mini album, and we use the space more efficiently.
However, as a general rule, full albums are released under the normal CD-DA format.

Q: What kind of data will be included?

A: We plan to record sound sources with higher quality than CDs (so-called Hi-Res). Basically, it is 48kHz/24bit FLAC.

Q: How do I listen to that?

A: The music area can be played on CD-DA compatible devices such as CD players, CD-playable components or radio cassette players, DVD players, and game consoles.
In addition to normal sound sources, you can play back sound and video recorded in the data area on a computer using general-purpose player software.

  • There are cases that only the data area is displayed in file explorer, but you can play the music area with various music player software.
  • On Macintosh and Linux/GNU/X, the CD-DA area and the data area may be displayed separately, but there is no problem with the operation.

Q: Can I rip the sound source from CD-EXTRAs?

A: It has been confirmed that CD-DA sources can be extracted by various ripping software. Sound sources in the data area can be copied and pasted from File Explorer to a fixed drive or USB memory.
※On the freedb server, Perhaps its tracks name are registered in English or the data area is registered as one song with the title of "extra data".

Q: Doesn't the sound quality get worse?

A: For CD-DA sources, there is no degradation of sound quality unless there is at least a C2 error (an error that does not go away after two corrections).
The sound source in the data area has a higher bit depth value than the normal sound source, so the sound quality is higher.

For Video Games

Q: Can I upload the play video of the NJB's video game?

A: As a general rule, it is prohibited. This is because it may contain works other than NJB.
Each work or label may have a different judgment. So in that case, please follow it.

Q: Can I make derivative works?

A: There is no restriction when it is produced as a hobby of an individual or a group of dojins, as long as it is considered not to be mistaken as authorised.
Please handle it according to the following regulations.


Examples of works that fall under derivative works
  • A picture drawn by your own hand, a manga, a figure made by your own hand, based on the graphics of the work
  • A novel or scenario based on the text of a work and with your own interpretation
  • Music data arranged from the music of a work (including compact discs, SA-CDs, distribution on the Internet, etc.; so-called 'tried to play' is also included in this category)
  • Video games based on the work
Examples of works that do not qualify as derivative works
  • Doujinshi magazines and various goods that directly quote the images in the work
  • Novels that quote verbatim from the text of the work
  • Music data taken directly from the work

Q: Can I quote materials in the product?

A: Please adhere to the following points.


Graphic Materials
  • The purpose of distributing materials is strictly prohibited.
  • Please specify the product name and label name of the citation source. You can embed it in the graphic.
  • For websites, you can use up to 5 graphics per page.
  • Please publish it in a size less than 50 percent of the original size.
  • Image processing is permitted except for changing the aspect ratio for reasons other than force majeure.
Text Materials
  • Please specify the product name and label name of the citation source.
Audio Materials
  • Please specify the product name and label name of the citation source.
  • In the case of MIDI music data, up to 3 songs per person are permitted.
  • In the case of sampled data, up to 2 songs per person are permitted.
  • The sampling frequency and bit depth are less than 44.1 kilohertz / 16 bits. The length of the song is 1/3 of the original length. Fade it out or cut it into chunks.

In addition, there is no problem as long as it happens to be photobombed, including game works.
