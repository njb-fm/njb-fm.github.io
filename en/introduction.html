INTRODUCTION

What's NJB?

In July 2017, NJB was established as the "Uchikawa Central Broadcasting Station" in order to provide FM microbroadcasting closer to the local community.
In 2018, the name was changed to Radio New Japan Broadcasting Club, and in February 2024, in order to concentrate on the software development business, and restarted as Naka-mura Jukebox.
What we are aiming for is local growth, and an insatiable quest.

Chronology

2017
07/09 Uchikawa Central Broadcasting Station was established.
On the same day, a preliminary license was issued (a lie).
09/09 Service broadcasting started at 23:00 with a transmission frequency of
89 megahertz and an output power of 0.05 microwatts.
11/11 As of this day, broadcasting was temporarily suspended.
2018
05/12 Test broadcasting resumpted.
07/01 Change the station name to Radio New Japan Broadcasting Club.
07/22 Radio New Japan (former club) was closed. At 24:00 on the same day,
the business was transferred to Radio New Japan (new club).
09/01 At 9:30, the frequency was changed to 77.7 MHz and the broadcasting started.
An ASMR program was broadcasted at midnight on the same day.
09/15 Broadcasting was suspended due to spurious and poor sound quality.
11/?? Illustrator Harukichi joins as a support member.
11/15 High-Games (now Ruritania) was established.
2019
02/02 Broadcasting resumed at 87.7 MHz. On the same day, output power was increased to 50 mW.
03/22 Broadcasting began at 87.7 MHz.
12/31 Broadcasting was suspended since this day due to the director's entrance examination for the kosen college.
2020
02/22 Broadcasting resumed after the director passed the entrance examination for the kosen college.
05/03 At the "Air Comiket" held from the same day, "Zer0: INVITATION" was distributed in PDF.
08/09 At 21:25, the test broadcast started at 77.7 MHz, and the output power was reduction to 0.01 milliwatts due to the adoption of a high-gain antenna.
08/15 Began two wave simultaneous broadcasting in anticipation of the migration to 77.7 MHz.
08/21 "Zer0" and "The Great Days" are converted to high-definition production.
12/30 At "Air Comiket 2" held for 2 days from the same day, "Zer0: INVITATION Revised Edition" was distributed in PDF.
2021
02/01 The stage name of the director was changed to "Jin Asa-nami".
03/27 At 23:00, transmission at 87.7 megahertz for non-emergency purposes was suspended.
04/03 Antenna power was increased to 750 microwatts.
5/5 The following items were distributed at Air Comiket 3.
●Kamuriki Linux 0.1.0
●ONE Piano Arrange Collection
5/8 Antenna power was increased to 1 milliwatts.
5/30 Antenna power was increased to 2.5 milliwatts.
The experiment of facsimile broadcasting using the main carrier was started.
6/13 A facsimile broadcasting experimental station was transferred to a 87.7 megahertz sub-carrier,
and the experiment was conducted in parallel with the text broadcasting experiment.
Later, music broadcasting experiment with sub carrier also began.
8/8 The multiplex broadcasting experiment was completed in preparation for the Digital Radio Mondiale (DRM) experiment.
10/2 DRM experimental broadcasting started and continued until December 25 of the same year.
12/5 Before the Air Comiket 4, Jin Asa-nami released his first original song collection "Great Days Image Soundtracks".
2022年
02/1 The program scheduling was drastically changed.
02/14 "High-Games" was renamed to "Ruritania" even though any works hadn't released from this label yet.
02/23 Kamuriki Linux 1.0 was released.
02/25 Kamuriki Linux 1.4 was released.
03/17 Opened an NJB channel on IRCNet, but it closed in a few days due to lack of people.
03/18 Broadcasting was suspended until March 31 in order to update the equipment.
03/26 Pre-broadcasting started at a frequency of 88.1 megahertz and the output power of 50 milliwatts.
04/01 Broadcasting began.
07/09 Fifth anniversary of the foundation.
09/09 Fifth anniversary of its starting broadcast.
2023
02/12 Broadcasting was suspended until March 12 under the name of 'empty period'.
03/12 After the expiration of the 'empty period', broadcasting was resumed.
05/14 The frequency was changed again to 77.7 megahertz, and the programme schedule was drastically changed to the one format Oto no Seseragi' (Murmuring of Sound).
05/21 As 87.7 megahertz became stable, broadcasting was resumed and RDS radiotext was also started.
10/15 From 21:30, the frequency was changed again to 77.7 megahertz, and at the same time, RDS radiotext was suspended.
2024
02/01 Club name changed to "Naka-mura Jukebox".
03/31 FM microbroadcasting is scheduled to end.
